BMI provides a general indication of whether an individual's weight falls within a healthy range. Here are the common BMI categories and their interpretations:

Underweight:

BMI less than 18.5
Indicates that a person may be undernourished or have a health issue causing low body weight.

Normal Weight:

BMI between 18.5 and 24.9
Indicates a healthy weight range for most individuals.

Overweight:

BMI between 25 and 29.9
Suggests that a person may have excess body weight, which can be associated with health risks.

Obese:

BMI of 30 or greater
Indicates a significantly higher body weight, and the individual may face increased health risks associated with obesity.
